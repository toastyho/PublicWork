Red-Eyed Tree Frog

Named after a Yu-Gi-Oh card

Care Level: Moderate


Price Range: 40$-200$ (USD)


Lifespan: 5-10 yrs


Diet: Crickets, worms, other insects


Compatibility: Other tree frogs

Profile Overview

With their beady red eyes, bright green skin, blue and yellow body patterns, and orange webbed feet, red-eyed tree frogs are quite the captivating species among the anura group. They naturally reside in tropical areas such as Costa Rica, Nicaragua, and Panama. Red-eyes are skittish due to them being prey to many animals in their natural habitat. They are nocturnal, but that doesn't mean you won't get to see them in action when you're awake. These frogs are active, which gives them the drive to move around and explore their surroundings.

As said earlier, red-eyd tree frogs are skittish animals, so handling them in a delicate manner is a must. This doesn't mean that you can't get close to it and observe its behaviour. They tend to activate their flight system through threatening sounds and/or physical contact.

Environment Overview

  • Capacity: 15-20 gallon tank
  • Temperature: 24℃- 30℃
  • Humidity: 60%- 80%

Red-eyed tree frogs thrive in rainforest environments, so their domestic habitat needs to replicate that tropical climate. The tank needs to be large in terms of height and overall size, since they like to move and jump around a lot. Large aboreal enclosures are the best choice for tree frogs. The water source should be big enough for tree frogs to soak in, but not enough to swim in (swimming leads to drowning for them). In rainforest settings, the temperature and humidity levels change a good amount throughout the cycle of a day. To ensure health of a red-eyed tree frog, drop the temperature and humditity levels to 21℃ and approximately 60% humditity at night time. To help with moderating these levels, the purchase of a digital thermometer is recomended. Tree frogs require UVB lighting as well.

NOTE: Make sure to do plenty of research on their habitat construction as it is essential for their survival

Tree frogs in general, are sociable creatures, so it is fine for you to have other tree frogs (same or different) in the same enclosure as your red-eyed tree frog. Their fearful nature only kicks in if you have other pets- they can cause tree frogs stress. If you are planning to have more than one tree frog in an enclosure, make sure the habitat itself is big enough to accomodate these active animals. Each tree frog should have their own space.

All the information presented here is just an overview. It's recommended that you do futher research into these species of frogs if you are interested in adopting them.
