Bumblebee Dart Frog

buzz buzz

Care Level: Easy


Price Range: 45$-80$ (USD)


Lifespan: 10+ yrs


Diet: Fireflies, isopods, springtails


Compatibility: Only their own species (male/female pairs)

Profile Overview

The bumblebee poison dart frog is known to be curious and somewhat intelligent by nature. They enjoy exploring their srurroundings, making them a very active species of frog. Their colour scheme resembles that of a bumblebee (may be the reason why they got their name) and they have wide flattened feet that are suitable for climbing and gripping. Among the poison dart frogs that exist, bumblebees are considered one of the loudest, so do take that into consideration when deciding on which frog to adopt. However, they are diurnal (active during the day), which means noise at night won't be a problem.

As its full name suggests, the bumblebee posion dart frog does secrete a toxin through its skin. Apparently, the toxin also tastes really bad (shoutout to whoever bit that bullet). The toxin does weaken over time in captivity, but the fact remains that they have delicate skin. Therefore, the physical handling of these frogs should be kept to a minimum. There isn't much need of looking after them anyway, given their intelligent nature.

Environment Overview

  • Capacity: 10-15 gallon tank per adult pair
  • Temperature: 21℃- 24℃
  • Humidity: +80%

Naturally found in the rainforest terrain of Venezuela, bumblebee dart frogs are found near ponds of water and like to be off the ground. This means that aboreal enclosures are a good choice for their habitat. Rather than focusing on real plant decor, it's better to have facilities where bumblebees can climb and jump onto, along with a small pond within the tank. Bumblebees don't require a lot of light and also don't do well in higher temperatures. So ensure whatever light source you use to light up the tank does not generate excess heat.

Bumblebee dart frogs are social creatures, so it's recomended to adopt more than one of them. As the compatibility section states, bumblebees should be adopted in male/female pairs as females tend to get aggressive with other females. This means as they age, it's better to keep pairs separated. Given their poisonous physiology, it's better to not keep other species of frogs within the same environment.

All the information presented here is just an overview. It's recommended that you do futher research into these species of frogs if you are interested in adopting them.
