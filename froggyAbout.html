About Frogs

What Are Frogs?

There are 3 main categories of amphibians- one of which are called frogs. These creatures are commonly known for their slimy skin, bulging eyes, and incredible jumping ability. Frogs are one of the most diverse set of animals in the world, with over 6,000 different speices currently existing today. Most frogs are nocturnal creatures, which may explain why some of you may hear croaking in the middle of the night. Frogs are able to adapt in most environments, allowing them to reside in areas all around the world (besides Antarctica- who wants to live there anyway). The only restriction placed on their habitat is that they require a source of water for reproduction (and drinking obviously).

Appearance

Frogs are quadruped animals that tend to have skinny limbs attatched to a central body part composed of both the torso and the head. Yes, this means that frogs do not have a neck and therefore cannot turn their head. This is where their bulging eyes come in handy. Thanks to this unique physiological feature, frogs are able to see what's behind them without having to turn their entire body around.

As said earlier, frogs are known for their slimy skin and bulging eyes. The 'slime' is actually a mucous coating that keeps their skin moist, along with allowing them to breathe through their skin. To elaborate further (for my sophisticated reader), frog skin is semi-permiable, which means that things that make contact with their skin can be absorbed into their body. It's important to note that not all frogs have this booger jacket. Other frogs may secrete a wax-like coating around parts of their body that have high temperatures in order to reatin moisture in that area. There are frogs that also secrete a white toxic fluid. It usually comes out when the frog feels threatened. Of these frogs, some of them secrete a toxin so deadly that a single droplet of it would be enough to kill an adult human (so maybe don't threaten them).

What's also interesting about a frogs' skin is that its colours and patterns vary depending on the speices. Poisonous frogs tend to have bright colour schemes (red, yellow, pink, etc.) to warn predators that their skin is toxic. Some frogs that are not poisonous may also wear these colours to trick predators to stay away from them. Many frogs tend to have greenish and/or brownish coloured skin. However, frogs are capable of changing their skin colour to blend in better with their surroundings! This may come as a shock to some of you who thought only reptiles (namely, the chameleon) were capable of this feat. The colour change process isn't as effective as some reptiles, but it does do its intended purpose of protecting themselves from predators through the arts of camouflage. Their colour-changes aren't necessarily a response to their physical surroundings, but rather they usually occur as a reaction to a temperature change. There are a small number of speices that adjust their colour due to changes in light exposure and air moisture.

Ribbit?

Many of us in elementary school were taught that frogs croak and make the "ribbit" sound. While this is true, it isn't necessarily the case for all frogs. Rather than croaking, there are some frogs that can grunt, whistle, chirp, or even bark! So when asked what sound does a frog make, "woof" and "tweet tweet" are viable answers (but let's be honest- dogs and birds don't actually sound like that either).

All frog speices emit their own unique sound. There is a pocket of air called the vocal sac found around the throat area which vibrates the air inside as frogs let the air out. This causes the frog to produce a sound. The ability to croak is limited to male frogs, which they use to attract a mate. Female frogs listen to the frequencies and depth of a male frog's sound, and choose their mate based on what they like. This doesn't mean that females are incapable of making sound. Female frogs produce a very soft sound barely audible to the human ear. Other reasons as to why frogs croak is to proclaim marked territory, warn others of potential dangers, and/or display signs of distress/alarm.

Offspring

As part of the amphibian physiology, frogs reproduce by laying eggs. Frog eggs need to be incubated by having them submerged in water for up to 23 days (minimum 2 days for some species). Once a female frog is fully matured, they are capable of laying more than 50,000 (two is sufficent, however) eggs in one sitting ! The male frog typically protects the eggs during their incubation process.

Depending on the speices of frogs, they can mature into a full adult within 2 months to roughly 3 years. The maturity rate is also affected by the temperature of the environment they are being brought up on. As a newborn, frogs appear to be limbless fish-like creature with a tail. They are referred to as tadpoles, rather than baby frogs. Tadpoles need to be submerged in water up until they lose their "tail" and grow some legs. They then migrate to land, and continue on with what is assumed a happy froggy life.

So Then... What's A Toad?

Ah yes, toads. Many of us confuse them for frogs and vice versa. What makes the two so similar in order for us to use their names interchangeably? Well scientifically speaking, toads and frogs stem from the same anura(frog category of amphibians) family. Therefore, toads do share the same phisiology and habitual behaviours as frogs do. There are however, a few differences that allowed them to be given their own name. Here's a list of the key differences that can help you figure out whether an anura is either a frog or a toad.

Skin: Frogs have a sleek, smooth, and shiny skin texture. Whereas toads tend to have a lumpy skin texture (almost like warts). Frogs also tend to have brighter skin color.

Location: Both frogs and toads can reside in most areas, but toads are much more capable of withstanding dry conditions than frogs. So if you see an anura amphibian on the middle of a dry pavement road, chances are it's a toad.

Legs: Frogs have slim long legs that are better suited for hopping. Toads have shorter stubby legs. Given the option, toads prefer to crawl rather than hop.

Shape: Not only limited to the legs, frogs tend to have a more slim athletic build than a toad. Toads also have a wider nose. In other words, if it's a chubby boy, it's most likely a toad.

Eggs: Even before birth, you can determine what the creature is through their eggs! If the eggs are laid in a linear fashion, they belong to a toad. If the eggs are incubated in a clump, then they are frog eggs.
