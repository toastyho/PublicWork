Chubby Frog

The term "fat frog" was cancelled

Care Level: Easy


Price Range: 12$-30$ (USD)


Lifespan: 10+ yrs


Diet: Crickets, worms, other insects


Compatibility: Only their own species

Profile Overview

These docile animals are formerly known as Asian painted frogs, but have been given an informal (and colloquial) name through the affection of those involved in pet trade. Chubby frogs have big appetites so ensuring they have food available most of the day is recommended. They adapt to their surrounding environment very quickly even when the environment itself is not ideal (i.e. they aren't picky). Chubby frogs are nocturnal animals, but fret not- they aren't hyperactive so your sleep should not be affected. Overall, chubby frogs have low requirements making them very good choices to start off with if this is your first time adopting a frog.

Given their big appetite, chubby frogs may mistake your finger for food! Don't worry, their bites don't hurt (it's more cute if anything). Like most frogs, handling should be kept to a minimum. It's important to be gentle with them because when threatened, they will secrete an unpleasant mucous from its body. It isn't poisonous, but it does smell bad.

Environment Overview

  • Capacity:15 gallon tank
  • Temperature: 20℃- 26℃
  • Humidity: ~70%

Chubby frogs with their stubby limbs, aren't great at jumping. Instead, they like to climb and dig around for fun. Greater heights in a tank are ideal for chubby frogs (room for climbing), but are not necessary. As a rule of thumb, 10 inches high should be good enough. A simple terrestrial enclosure should be more than enough for a chubby frog. They adapt to cptivity easily, and therfore don't require a lot of commodities to survive. Having a water source just large enough for them to soak in, along with "diggable" land material are the only things that could be deemed necessary for them (pretty much things that all frogs need). Inclusion of hiding spots for chubby frogs will be better for them mentally.

Asian painted frogs get along with others of its own kind. They are docile frogs, so it is possible for them to get along with other species. However, chubby frogs are hungry animals, so it is also possible for them to eat other species (especially those that are smaller than it). It's best to just have chubby frogs associate with one another. If you are looking to breeding them, you will find better luck doing that in the wild than in a domestic environment.

All the information presented here is just an overview. It's recommended that you do futher research into these species of frogs if you are interested in adopting them.
