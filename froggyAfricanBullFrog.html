Africa Bullfrog

Has something to do with Africa. And bulls.

Care Level: Intermediate


Price Range: 25$-75$ (USD)


Lifespan: 15-25 yrs


Diet: Insects, rodents, fish, small birds, reptiles, other frogs


Compatibility: Very low

Profile Overview

Behold! One of the largest frog species known to exist; the African bullfrog! These big guys can weigh from 2 to 4 pounds, and eat just about anything that can fit in its mouth (given that it is an alive meat source). Given its diet, you may wonder how it's capable of eating certain creatures. The answer is that they has small razor-sharp teeth, unlike most frog species. When feeding them, usage of tongs is recommended to avoid bite to your finger (which will not only hurt you but also potentially make the frog sick). These frogs also have very strong legs with sharp calluses that give them incredible jumping and digging capabilities.

African bullfrogs are very relaxed in general. This means you wouldn't need to stimulate them much- they can just lay back without the need of your intervention. Like most frogs, handling African bullfrogs is a delicate procedure. What makes handling African bullfrogs more dangerous than other frogs is its physique described in the previous paragraph. These frogs are laid-back animals until they are provoked. Once rattled, they are willing to attack whatever provoked them or leap great distances to get away. So key tip: try not to make them angry... you wouldn't like them when they're angry.

Environment Overview

  • Capacity: 10 gallon tank
  • Temperature: 24℃
  • Humidity: +60%

Since African bullfrogs are laid-back animals, they don't need a climbing facilities in their habitat. They do require a body of water large enough for them to swim in. It's recommended to have roughly a third of the tank dedicated to fresh water. Another portion of the tank should be composed of dirt/rock so that they have an area to dry off. African bullfrogs also like to dig, so ensuring the dryland is large enough for excavation is a key consideration when building your habitat for them. Multiple zone environments make an excellent choice for African bullfrogs. Cleaning the tank frequently is necessary as the ammonia from their waste (or as the kids say- poop) can poison them.

These formidable frogs are not the best with regards to social compatibility. It's best to keep their social environment minimal. Having other African bullfrogs is acceptable, but that's it. African bullfrogs are hungry animals, and are capable of eating other frogs. The ideal social environment for an African bullfrog would be for it to be alone or have a single partner (that's also an African bullfrog).

All the information presented here is just an overview. It's recommended that you do futher research into these species of frogs if you are interested in adopting them.
